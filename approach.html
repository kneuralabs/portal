The five stages

How We Work.

Every Kneuralabs engagement follows this sequence — from full AI inventory through audit-ready evidence production.

01

Discover

We conduct structured interviews across IT, legal, operations, HR, and business unit leads. We review procurement records, vendor contracts, SaaS subscriptions, and internal build logs. Every AI system — built, bought, or embedded — is surfaced and catalogued.

Weeks 1–2
02

Diagnose

Each AI system is assessed against five governance dimensions: accountability, transparency, risk controls, monitoring, and third-party oversight. Risks are scored by likelihood and severity. Nothing is assumed. Everything is evidenced.

Weeks 2–4
03

Design

We design your governance infrastructure around how your business actually works — not a generic template. Policy suite, RACI matrix, lifecycle controls, and monitoring cadence are built to your sector, scale, and regulatory context.

Weeks 4–10
04

Deploy

We install the governance programme with your team — not for them. Every deliverable is documented for operational continuity. Every process is designed for handover. We build capability, not dependency.

Weeks 8–14
05

Defend

We assess your governance posture against your target standard — NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, or the EU AI Act — identify every gap, and produce the evidence packs, documented controls, and audit-ready dossiers that certifying bodies require.

Ongoing
KneuraLens™

How we look at AI governance.

The KneuraLens™ framework is the analytical lens we apply at every stage — four interlocking perspectives that together produce a complete picture of governance readiness.

I

Inventory Lens

A systematic view of every AI system — what it does, who owns it, what data it touches, and what would happen if it failed or was audited tomorrow.

  • System scope and function
  • Data flow and lineage
  • Ownership and accountability
  • Third-party dependencies
II

Risk Lens

Structured risk classification grounded in established frameworks — not gut feel. Every system rated against consistent criteria with documented rationale.

  • NIST AI RMF alignment
  • EU AI Act risk tier classification
  • Business impact analysis
  • Regulatory obligation mapping
III

Governance Lens

An assessment of the policies, structures, and processes that govern AI in your organisation — and where the gaps are.

  • Policy coverage assessment
  • Accountability gap analysis
  • Process maturity evaluation
  • Board and leadership visibility
IV

Readiness Lens

A forward-looking view of your organisation's capacity to maintain governance — and to demonstrate it to auditors, clients, and regulators.

  • Evidence pack quality review
  • Certification gap analysis
  • Team capacity assessment
  • Roadmap to audit-ready status
