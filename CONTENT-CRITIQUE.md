# Content Critique — Apple Editorial Standard

Reviewed: all 9 pages. Voice is strong — short sentences, confident verbs,
no AI-buzzword sludge. The defects below are the gap between "good B2B
copy" and "shippable."

## P0 — Ship-blocking

**1. British spelling on a U.S.-targeted site.** 65 instances:
`programme` (34), `organis-` (29), `prioritis-` (2). The site sells to
"U.S. mid-market"; the copy says *programme*, *organisation*,
*prioritised*. A governance firm that can't keep its own house
consistent undercuts every claim of rigour. Global replace →
`program`, `organization`, `prioritized`.

**2. Verbatim triad repeated two lines apart (about.html).**
Mission heading: "Governance that is clear, defensible, and built to
last." Next sentence: "Every mid-market business running AI deserves
governance that is clear, defensible, and built to last." Apple never
echoes the headline in the body. Keep the heading; rewrite the line.

## P1 — Credibility & consistency

**3. Same person, two number styles, same page (about.html).**
"Piyal Gupta spent **fifteen years**..." vs. bio "**15+ years** building
governance strategy for Fortune 500." Pick one (Apple: spell out under
ten, numerals above — so "15 years").

**4. "Fifteen years building AI risk frameworks" strains math.** NIST AI
RMF (2023) and the EU AI Act (2024) are new. For a firm whose product
*is* defensibility, an indefensible claim is the worst possible tell.
Reframe: "fifteen years in enterprise risk — the last several building
AI governance frameworks."

**5. Contraction voice wobbles.** "We do not write policies that sit in
drawers" / "We don't compete on price" / "We are looking" / "We're in
the process" — all within the same register. Apple is consistently
contracted and warm. Standardize on "we don't / we're."

## P2 — Polish

**6. CTA verbs sprawl.** "Get in touch," "Book discovery call," "Request
discovery call," "Talk to a lead," "Express interest," "Request S1."
Collapse to one primary ("Book a discovery call") + one secondary.

**7. "Holds" is doing undefined work.** "something that holds,"
"Governance that holds" (×2) — a recurring tic that never resolves to a
concrete promise. Either define it once or replace with the specific
outcome (audit-ready, board-readable).

**8. Mild flag-waving.** "building America's future" /
"the governance layer for America's AI economy." Apple is confident,
not patriotic. Tighten to the customer's outcome, not the nation's.

**9. Verify HTML entities render.** `&trade;`, `&rarr;`, `&amp;` appear
in source repeatedly — confirm they paint as ™ → & and aren't
double-escaped in the live DOM.

## What's already Apple-grade — keep

- "Built to run. Not to file." — best line on the site.
- "The AI exposure was identical. The regulatory pressure was
  identical. The governance infrastructure was not." — earned staccato.
- "We build governance programmes that run." (fix spelling, keep cadence)
- Service deliverables (D.01–D.06): concrete, scannable, no fluff.

**Priority order:** Fix #1 and #2 before anything ships. #3–5 are
one editing pass. #6–9 are a polish sprint.
